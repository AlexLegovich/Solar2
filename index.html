    496
  • Total power
    327 kW
  • Energy cover

496 solar panels with a total output of 327.4 kW were installed in Brūveri, Brunava Parish. The solar panels will generate 317.1 MWh of clean power for the pig farming facility every year, preventing 95 tonnes of CO2 from entering the atmosphere, an equivalent of twenty-three AIRBUS A321 flights between Riga and Tallinn. The system covers some 25% of the overall annual power consumption of the facility, taking up 5000 m2 of land.

Ošukalns wood processing facility in Jēkabpils
  • Country
    Latvia
  • Panel amount
    1050
  • Total power
    865 kw
  • Energy cover
    20%

Ošukalns, a diversified company, has installed 1050 solar panels with a total system capacity of 425 kW on the roof of its wood processing plant in Jēkabpils, which should produce nearly 386 MWh of green electricity per year. On an annual basis, this accounts for 25% of the company's electricity self-consumption, or over than 70% during the summer months. This green electricity will be used in-house, to power the wood planing and heat treatment (thermodryer) processes at the plant. Every year, this will reduce atmospheric CO2 emissions by 116.6 tonnes, or the equivalent of 12 trips around the globe by car.
